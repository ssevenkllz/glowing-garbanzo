# ZERO CORP — TRAVEL & EXPENSE POLICY HANDBOOK
## Section 3: Approvals

### 3.1 When Approval Is Required
Not all travel requires the same level of approval. Zero Corp uses a tiered approval model based on trip cost and destination type.

| Trip Type | Estimated Total Cost | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic — Standard | Under $1,500 | Direct Manager |
| Domestic — Extended or high-cost | $1,500–$5,000 | Direct Manager + Finance |
| International — Standard | Any amount | Direct Manager + VP |
| International — High Risk (Level 2+) | Any amount | Direct Manager + VP + Security |
| Emergency/Same-Week Travel | Any amount | Direct Manager + VP |
| Conference/Event Registration | Over $500 | Direct Manager |

Approval must be obtained **before booking**. Retroactive approval requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis and may be denied.

### 3.2 Approval Chain by Role
The following approval chains apply based on the traveler's role:

**Individual Contributors and Associates:**
- Manager → (if required) Finance Business Partner → (if required) VP of Department

**Managers and Senior Managers:**
- VP of Department → (if required) CFO

**Directors and Above:**
- CFO → CEO (for trips over $10,000)

If your direct manager is the one traveling, approval escalates to their manager automatically.

### 3.3 How to Submit an Approval Request
The Zero Corp Travel Copilot handles approval submission automatically when you initiate a trip. Alternatively, approvals can be submitted manually:

1. Log in to Concur Travel (concur.zerocorp.com)
2. Click "New Trip Request"
3. Enter destination, travel dates, estimated costs, and business purpose
4. Attach any supporting documentation (client meeting invite, conference registration, etc.)
5. Submit — approvers are notified automatically via email and Slack

The Travel Copilot can prepare your approval request for you. Just tell it your destination, dates, and purpose — it will fill in the rest.

### 3.4 Approval Turnaround Time Expectations
Zero Corp sets the following SLA expectations for approval responses:

- **Standard domestic requests**: 1 business day
- **International or high-cost requests**: 2 business days
- **Emergency/same-week requests**: 4 hours (escalated automatically if not responded to)

If an approver does not respond within the SLA window, the request is automatically escalated to the approver's manager. The Travel Desk (travel@zerocorp.com) can also assist in escalating stalled approvals.

### 3.5 What To Do If Approval Is Denied or Delayed
**If denied:**
- You will receive an email from Concur with the reason for denial
- Review the denial reason — common issues include: missing business justification, exceeding cost thresholds, or incomplete documentation
- You may revise and resubmit the request within 48 hours
- If you believe the denial is in error, contact your manager directly and copy the Travel Desk

**If delayed beyond SLA:**
- The Copilot will notify you if your approval is past SLA
- You may also contact the Travel Desk to manually escalate
- For time-sensitive trips, request VP-level override through your manager

### 3.6 Emergency Travel — Expedited Approval Process
When business travel is required with less than 48 hours notice:

1. Contact your manager directly (phone or Slack — do not rely on email alone)
2. Obtain verbal approval and document it in writing (a Slack message or email confirmation is sufficient)
3. Contact the Travel Desk at (800) 555-0192 (24/7) for emergency booking assistance
4. Submit the formal approval request in Concur within 24 hours of booking
5. Attach the documented verbal approval to the Concur request

Emergency travel that is booked without any manager awareness — verbal or written — is not reimbursable.
