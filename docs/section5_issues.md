# ZERO CORP — TRAVEL & EXPENSE POLICY HANDBOOK
## Section 5: Issues & Exceptions

### 5.1 How to Report a Travel Disruption
When something goes wrong during travel, Zero Corp wants to know quickly so we can help. Report disruptions through any of the following channels:

**Via Copilot:** Tell the Travel Copilot what happened — it will triage the situation and connect you with the right resource.

**Via Travel Desk:** Call (800) 555-0192 (24/7) or email travel@zerocorp.com. For urgent situations, call — do not email.

**Via Concur:** Log the disruption in your trip record under "Trip Issues" within 24 hours of the event.

What to include in your report:
- What happened and when
- Your current location
- What actions you've already taken
- What you need right now

### 5.2 Out-of-Policy Spend — Exception Request Process
Sometimes circumstances require spending outside of policy — a sold-out hotel in your price range, a last-minute flight at a premium, an unexpected client dinner. Zero Corp has a clear process for these situations.

**Pre-trip exceptions** (before the expense occurs — preferred):
1. Submit an exception request through Concur under "Policy Exception"
2. Include: what you need to spend, why it's necessary, and what alternatives were considered
3. Travel Desk reviews within 4 hours (same business day)
4. You'll receive email approval or a request for more info

**Post-trip exceptions** (after the expense — use sparingly):
1. Submit expense in Concur as normal
2. Flag it as "Policy Exception" and include a written explanation
3. Your manager and Finance will review during expense approval
4. Unexplained out-of-policy expenses will be flagged for review and may be denied

Repeated out-of-policy spend without documented exceptions may result in revocation of corporate card privileges.

### 5.3 Medical Emergencies Abroad
In the event of a medical emergency while traveling internationally:

**Immediate steps:**
1. Call local emergency services (112 in EU, 999 in UK, 911 in North America)
2. Get to the nearest hospital or medical facility
3. Call Zero Corp's 24/7 Emergency Line: (800) 555-0911
4. Call Corporate Amex emergency assistance (back of card) — they provide medical evacuation coordination if needed

**Coverage:**
- Zero Corp's corporate Amex provides emergency medical coverage including hospitalization, emergency evacuation, and repatriation
- Keep all medical receipts and documentation
- File reimbursement through Concur within 30 days of return with full medical documentation

**Notifying Zero Corp:**
- Notify your manager as soon as you are safely able
- HR will be notified by the Emergency Line team and will reach out regarding any needed support or leave

### 5.4 Security Incidents — Who to Contact
**If you feel unsafe or witness a security incident:**

1. **Remove yourself from danger first** — your safety is the priority
2. **Call local authorities** if there is immediate danger (police, 112, 999, etc.)
3. **Call Zero Corp Security**: (800) 555-0199 (24/7)
4. **Do not post about the incident on social media** until cleared by Security team
5. **Document everything** once you are safe: time, location, what happened, who was involved

Zero Corp Security will coordinate with local authorities, your manager, HR, and Legal as appropriate. You will not be asked to manage this alone.

**For non-emergency security concerns** (suspicious activity, data security while traveling, etc.):
- Email security@zerocorp.com with details
- Response within 2 hours during business hours, 4 hours off-hours

**The golden rule:** When in doubt, call the Travel Desk first. They are trained to triage any situation and get you to the right person fast.
