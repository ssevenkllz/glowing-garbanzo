# ZERO CORP — TRAVEL & EXPENSE POLICY HANDBOOK
## Section 1: Travel Planning & Preparation

### 1.1 Who Needs to Travel and When
Business travel at Zero Corp is authorized when the purpose of travel directly supports a client engagement, internal project, approved conference, or executive directive. Travel should only be initiated when the business objective cannot be reasonably achieved through remote means (video conference, phone, etc.).

All travel must be initiated by the employee and approved by their direct manager prior to booking. Travel booked without prior authorization may not be reimbursed.

### 1.2 Booking Windows and Lead Time Requirements
Zero Corp requires the following minimum lead times for travel booking:

- **Domestic travel**: Book at least 7 days in advance
- **International travel**: Book at least 21 days in advance
- **Same-week/emergency travel**: Requires VP-level approval and must be booked through the Zero Corp Travel Desk (travel@zerocorp.com)

Booking outside these windows without documented approval may result in out-of-policy flagging and delayed reimbursement.

### 1.3 Preferred Vendors
Zero Corp has negotiated rates with the following preferred vendors. Employees are expected to book through preferred vendors unless a documented exception is approved.

**Airlines**: United, Delta, American Airlines (via Concur Travel portal)
**Hotels**: Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt (via Concur Travel portal)
**Car Rental**: Enterprise, Hertz
**Ride Share**: Uber for Business, Lyft Business (linked to Zero Corp account)
**Booking Platform**: Concur Travel (concur.zerocorp.com)

### 1.4 Destination Risk Assessment
All international travel destinations are assessed using the U.S. Department of State Travel Advisory system:

- **Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions)**: Pre-approved, no additional steps required
- **Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution)**: Manager approval required; traveler must register with the State Department STEP program
- **Level 3 (Reconsider Travel)**: VP approval required; Security team briefing mandatory
- **Level 4 (Do Not Travel)**: Travel not authorized under any standard circumstances; requires C-Suite approval and Security sign-off

Current travel advisories are available at travel.state.gov.

### 1.5 International Travel Requirements
Employees traveling internationally are responsible for ensuring they have:

- A valid passport (must not expire within 6 months of return date)
- Required visas for the destination country
- Any required vaccinations or health documentation
- Travel insurance (provided automatically through Zero Corp's corporate Amex card)
- Registration with the U.S. State Department STEP program for Level 2+ destinations

Zero Corp's Travel Desk can assist with visa guidance but cannot apply on behalf of the employee. Allow 4-6 weeks for visa processing where required.

**Key international destinations and visa requirements** are maintained in the Zero Corp Travel Portal under "International Resources."

### 1.6 Pre-Trip Checklist
The Zero Corp Travel Copilot automatically generates a pre-trip checklist upon booking confirmation. Standard checklist items include:

- [ ] Trip approved by manager
- [ ] Booking confirmed in Concur
- [ ] Passport/ID valid and accessible
- [ ] Visa obtained (international)
- [ ] Hotel confirmation saved
- [ ] Ground transportation arranged
- [ ] Emergency contacts saved (see Section 4.7)
- [ ] Out-of-office set
- [ ] Expense reporting profile updated in Concur
